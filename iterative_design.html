Iterative Design Process

  1. Initial Prototype: After exploring design options, I developed a starting point for the coding websites using Figma. This initial prototype served as a foundation for gathering feedback.
  2. User Feedback and Surveys: I didn't stop at the initial design. Throughout the development process, I actively utilized surveys at different stages:
    • Group Project Surveys: These surveys helped me understand broader design preferences and user needs, which I could then apply to my own portfolio.
    • Surveys During and After Prototypes: These targeted surveys focused specifically on my web-design prototypes. User feedback received through these surveys helped identify areas for improvement and refine the design for optimal user experience.
  3. Iteration and Refinement: Based on the feedback received from the surveys, I iteratively improved the design of my portfolio. This involved making changes to the layout, UI elements, and potentially even content based on user suggestions.
  4. Final Post-Development Survey: After incorporating the feedback from previous iterations, I plan to conduct a final survey to ensure the complete portfolio website meets user expectations and effectively showcases my work.

What Benefits I See of Iterative Design

  • Gather valuable user insights: Surveys allowed me to understand how users interacted with the prototype and identify areas for improvement.
  • Make informed design decisions: User and teacher feedback provided a data-driven basis for refining the design and ensuring it aligns with user needs.
  • Create user-centered products: Iterative design ensured the final website offers an intuitive and engaging experience for visitors.

Conclusion

In conclusion, my portfolio development process demonstrates a strong grasp of iterative design principles. Utilizing professional design tools like Figma, coupled with consistent user feedback through surveys, allowed me to create a portfolio website that is not only visually appealing but also optimized for user experience. This is a testament to the effectiveness of the iterative design approach.

In short terms: Iterative Design in my eyes, is one of the most important things I learned and am currently studying in Fontys, really differentiating me and making me professional.
