Portfolio Project - User Testing 1 (Quantitative Data)

I performed multiple user-tests including gathering quantitative data and peer feedbacks from students and teacher feedbacks:

Quantitative Data (Full Research Results)

Goals of Research:

Gather quantitative data regarding the feeling of a customer towards the website. We gather quantitative data, due to the fact that when a person goes over a survey marking different numbers, their brain falls into an autopilot state, forcing them to not think and give more authentic answers (My teacher Guido taught me that).

Main Questions:

  • What device do the users mostly use?
  • How do they navigate to and find specific information on the website?
  • Find specific content like learning outcomes. What are the users' preferences regarding colours?

Subquestions:

  • Does navigation feel intuitive?
  • How easy were x and y experiences?

Methodology:

Gather quantitative data by forcing the user to go into authentic autopilot by repetitively filling numbers.

Results:

  • Most users browse on desktops.
  • Most people prefer a minimalistic theme.
  • Most people rate finding different information as very easy.
  • Most people find navigating between the pages very intuitive.
  • Most users find the text readability a 9/10.
  • The majority of users find the website’s cleanness and professional feel an 8/10.
  • Additionally, a user was confused on how to go back to the main page from learning outcomes.

Portfolio Project - User Testing 2 (Peer and Teacher Feedback)

Main Questions:

  • What device do the users mostly use?
  • How do they navigate to and find specific information on the website?
  • Find specific content like learning outcomes. What are the users' preferences regarding colours?

Subquestions:

  • Does navigation feel intuitive?
  • How easy were x and y experiences?

Methodology:

Walk people through the prototype, analyse their behaviour, and write the most common problems/points of improvement encountered.

Results:

  • Give access to check the different learning outcomes on the main page.
  • Make them more dynamic, like images on each learning outcome leading to different pages, instead of just text.
  • Since the space in the prototype is too small, make a different HTML page for each learning outcome.
  • Instead of having many different GitHub icons in the background on the main page, make one big to differentiate that it's a link because otherwise, it's confusing and seems like nothing more than a background design.
  • Add dark theme (if the time is enough).
  • Add an indication that you should scroll down.
  • Code a little about me section.
