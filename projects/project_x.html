Branding and Prototyping

Figma Website Wireframe Prototype and Branding - Final Prototype (link)

Methodology:

In order to create the branding and prototype, I used multiple DOT Framework methods. I utilised Available Product Analysis, Best good and bad practices, Prototyping, Peer review, and Usability Testing.

Available Product Analysis, Best good and bad practices

Analysed Top Industry Brand’s websites like Gymshark, XXL Nutrition, Lululemon, and UA to identify industry trends, common patterns, and strategies between the websites and adapted everything into one.

Top Takeaway was a common pattern that I found in the design layouts, which was proven to convert and I decided to apply into my own version.

Prototyping

The creation of wireframes using Figma is aligned with rapid prototyping methods to visualize and iterate on design concepts.

To make sure my Figma and prototyping skills were polished, since I had some insecurity in my UI/UX skills, I completed a course on UI/UX with Figma by Codecademy.

In the course, I learned a lot about UI/UX, Figma tricks, key methodologies, the 5 stages of iteration process - brainstorm, define, design, test and launch as well as user-centered design and the ten usability heuristics.

As mentioned above, the prototype is made with the analysis and best practices research, as well as feedback from teachers (registered as feedpulses) and peers.

Logo and Final Branding

With the help of AI and Photoshop, as well as teacher feedbacks I finished the brand's products images and banners.

For the branding, I have used multiple resources both from online research and Fontys lectures. I chose a black and white minimalistic theme, that is used by many big brands like Apple. And as an accent color, I did research - the purple color

Goals of Research:

Find appropriate colors and fonts

Question:

Which color makes most people feel special, upper-class, luxurious (excluding black and white)

Subquestions:

Should I use exactly black and exactly white?

Methodology:

Browse online marketing and psychology articles as well as newspapers

Result:

Minimalism, the purple color, as well as not having exactly black or white, but closer shades

Sources:

  • Neurofied
  • National Institute of Health
  • Help Scout
